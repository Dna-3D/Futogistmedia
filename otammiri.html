The FUTO community was thrown into mourning following the tragic death of Sigmus Chisom Onwuadikaije, a 200 level student of the Architecture Department, whose body was discovered in the Otamiri River. The heartbreaking incident has raised serious concerns about student safety and public awareness around river environments on and around campus.

What Happened?

According to reports, Sigmus Chisom went missing before his body was later found in the Otamiri River. While investigations are ongoing, early indications point strongly to drowning. Rivers like Otamiri may appear calm on the surface, but they often hide strong undercurrents, sudden depth changes, and slippery banks that can easily overpower even a healthy young adult.

Why Did His Body Look That Way?

Many people were shocked by the physical condition of the body when images surfaced online. It is important to clarify that the appearance was not caused by violence or an attack, but by natural post-mortem changes that occur when a body stays in water.

  • Water Decomposition: After death, bacteria inside the body produce gases. These gases cause the body to swell, especially the face, neck, and limbs.
  • Skin Breakdown: Prolonged exposure to water softens the skin, making it peel or appear distorted.
  • Water Pressure & Currents: River movement can reposition limbs unnaturally and cause swelling in certain areas.
  • Aquatic Activity: Small aquatic organisms may affect exposed areas like the lips or eyes after death.

These changes are medically documented and unfortunately common in drowning cases. While disturbing to see, they do not indicate foul play.

A Message to the FUTO Community

This tragic loss is a painful reminder that life is fragile and that certain environments, especially rivers, should never be underestimated.

Safety Precautions Students Must Take

  • Avoid swimming or bathing in rivers: Especially unsupervised or unfamiliar areas.
  • Do not walk alone near riverbanks: Slippery soil and erosion can cause sudden falls.
  • Avoid rivers at night: Visibility is low and help may not be readily available.
  • Stay away during heavy rains: River currents become stronger and unpredictable.
  • Report dangerous spots: Inform school authorities or student leaders about unsafe areas.

Final Words

Sigmus Chisom Onwuadikaije was a promising young student with dreams and a future ahead of him. His death is a devastating loss to his family, friends, department, and the entire FUTO community.
